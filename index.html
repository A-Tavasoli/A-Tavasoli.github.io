About

I am an Assistant Professor at James Madison University. My research focuses on nonlinear dynamical systems, control, optimization, and learning-based methods, with an emphasis on principled theory and mathematically grounded algorithm design.

My work spans adaptive control, nonlinear stability theory, contraction-inspired methods, online optimization, and control-oriented learning, with applications including automated insulin delivery and other complex engineered systems.

Primary Interests
Nonlinear control theory, Adaptive control, Nonlinear analysis tools, Nonlinear dynamics and chaos, First-order methods in optimization, online learning
Applications
Mechanical engineering vehicles and robotics
Approach
My research combines first-principles approaches from mechanical engineering and control theory with advanced nonlinear stability and convergence analysis for ODEs, PDEs, and optimization problems.

Research

I work on mathematically grounded problems in control, dynamics, and optimization, especially where stability, adaptation, and learning interact.

Adaptive and Nonlinear Control

Control design, stability analysis, adaptation laws, and rigorous treatment of nonlinear systems under uncertainty and feedback.

Adaptive control Stability Nonlinear systems

Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos

Dynamical behavior in nonlinear systems, including qualitative structure, complexity, and geometric viewpoints motivated by chaos and long-term behavior.

Chaos Dynamics Geometry

Contraction and Non-Euclidean Methods

Stability and learning through contractive vector fields, non-Euclidean metrics, and structure-aware methods for analysis and adaptation.

First-Order Methods for Online Learning and Control

Optimization-based perspectives on learning and control, especially first-order algorithms for sequential decision-making, online adaptation, and feedback design.
